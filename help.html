Help & FAQ

Q: How is the market prediction made?
A: It uses 24h market cap percentage changes to determine bullish or bearish momentum.

Q: How do I use the dashboard?
A: Simply visit the home page, and you’ll see trends visualized with Chart.js.

Q: Where does the crypto data come from?
A: All crypto market data is fetched live from the CoinGecko API, ensuring accurate and up-to-date information.

Q: Can I save my favorite coin?
A: Yes! Use the preferences form on the homepage to enter your username and favorite coin, which will be saved using our Supabase backend.

Q: Why don’t some coins show historical data?
A: If historical data is unavailable or incomplete for a coin, the chart will not display properly. We rely on CoinGecko’s dataset for this.

Q: How do I read the charts?
A: Bar charts show current prices of multiple coins. Line charts (displayed when clicking a coin) show the coin’s price trend over the last 30 days.

Q: What are bull and bear signals?
A: A bull signal means the market is rising (positive momentum), while a bear signal indicates it's declining (negative momentum), based on 24-hour price changes.
